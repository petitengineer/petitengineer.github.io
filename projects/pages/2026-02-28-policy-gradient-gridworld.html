Policy Gradient vs Value-Based RL in JAX

Assignment 2 · ELG5214 · University of Ottawa

Using Gymnasium, I implemented a 7×7 gridworld where the agent starts at \((1,1)\) and aims for \((5,5)\) with four actions (up, down, left, right). Observations include normalized coordinates, Manhattan distance to the goal, and goal direction. Rewards encourage shorter paths, penalize moving away from the goal, and apply large bonuses/penalties at the goal and in pits (which reset the agent to start).

DQN and REINFORCE-with-baseline were compared over 10 seeds, learning rates \(\{0.001, 0.01, 0.1\}\), and discount factors \(\gamma \in \{0.9, 0.99\}\) for 200 episodes (max 100 steps). A second study added a vertical pit column at \(x=2, y \in \{0,\ldots,4\}\).

DQN was sensitive to \(\gamma\) and learning rate—lr=0.1 failed badly, and \(\gamma=0.99\) often increased noise except at lr=0.001. REINFORCE preferred smaller learning rates but was less sensitive to \(\gamma\). JAX’s pure functions required explicit parameter/state threading and made vmap/jit natural for batched evaluation. Pits had limited effect on learning trends beyond lowering the random baseline.
