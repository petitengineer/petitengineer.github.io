Cross-Modal Dual-Agent RL for Emotion-Aligned Poetry and Music

CSI5340 / ELG5214 · University of Ottawa · Group 4

We built a cross-modal recommender that aligns poetry (Goodreads) and music (Million Song Dataset subset) in a shared 128-dimensional emotion space, then trains two cooperating RL agents to rerank short candidate slates. Text is encoded with DistilBERT, projected into a joint space anchored by valence–arousal signals, and trained with a triplet objective so neighborhoods reflect mood rather than surface wording.

Each modality has a lightweight world model (3-layer MLP on concatenated mood and item embeddings) trained on VA-distance synthetic feedback, plus an item-conditional slate policy optimized with REINFORCE and PPO against that simulator. A Gradio demo logs like/dislike clicks for inference-time taste vectors without retraining.

The triplet loss pulls same-mood cross-modal pairs together with margin \(\alpha = 0.2\):

\(\displaystyle \mathcal{L}_{\text{trip}} = \max\bigl(0,\; \|f(x)-f(x^+)\|_2 - \|f(x)-f(x^-)\|_2 + \alpha \bigr)\)

Policies score each slate item with an item-conditional softmax. Music reward blends listen time, cosine alignment, diversity, and skip probability (\(\alpha{=}0.35\), \(\beta{=}0.35\), \(\gamma{=}0.20\), \(\delta{=}0.10\)). Evaluation on 500 rollouts shows learned rerankers reach 0.42 mean reward vs. 0.38 for cosine/MMR on music, with WM retrieval at 0.43.
